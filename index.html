Our story

How We Got Here

Meghan and Owen met soon after college, by a small accident of circumstance: their roommates were dating each other. Meghan barely knew Edie when she moved in — and very nearly didn’t live with her at all. Everything that followed turns on that one near-miss.

Owen was taken with her from the start, and after a few months of friendship he said as much. One week into dating, Meghan told her friend Sophia, “I’m going to marry him.” That was five years ago.

Anyone who knows them will tell you they balance each other. Meghan is funny, exacting, and gloriously organized; Owen is calm, unhurried, quietly romantic, and every bit as funny. Between them, life manages to feel both lively and easy.

“He came halfway around the world to ask. I’d known the answer for years.”

At sunset · on a cliff above the Aegean

What Meghan didn’t know was that Owen was coming. While she was on a Greek island with Sophia, her family, and Edie — the roommate who had started all of it — he flew thirty-six hours to reach her. Her friends were in on it; so was Sophia’s mother, who spent an entire day quietly showing Owen around the island. He appeared at sunset on their second-to-last night, the blue-domed Church of the Seven Martyrs floating in the Aegean behind them.

She said yes. What she remembers just as clearly is how many people had quietly conspired to be there for it.
