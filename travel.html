Getting here

It’s a little out of the way. That’s rather the point.

Whitehouse Station sits in rural Hunterdon County — rolling farmland, about an hour west of Newark. Most guests will fly into Newark, rent a car, and drive in. Here’s everything you’ll need to plan the trip. (Hotel links below are placeholders until our blocks are confirmed.)

By air & by road

Best airport

Newark · EWR

The closest major hub — roughly a 1-hour drive to Whitehouse Station, traffic permitting. Direct flights from most of the country and much of Europe.

Also workable

Philadelphia · PHL

About 1 hr 15 min away, and sometimes cheaper. JFK and LaGuardia are doable too (≈ 1 hr 30 min) if the fare is right.

On the ground

Rent a car

You’ll want one out here — the venue and hotels are spread across the countryside and rideshares are scarce. Grab a car at the airport.

When to arrive

Thursday or Friday AM

It’s a Friday wedding, so plan to be settled by Friday midday. Thursday-night arrivals get the most relaxed start.

Where to stay

A few good beds, near the barn.

We’re holding room blocks at the hotels below. Do book early — Hunterdon fills up on summer weekends, and a Friday wedding means many of us arriving at once.

If you have a spare afternoon

Things to do nearby.

Taste

Wine & cider trails

Hunterdon is quietly full of small vineyards and orchards. Old York Cellars and Beneduce are easy, pretty stops.

Wander

Lambertville & New Hope

Twin river towns on the Delaware — antiques, galleries, a footbridge between two states, and dinner with a view.

Walk

Round Valley

A startlingly blue reservoir ringed by trails. Bring good shoes; the color of the water alone is worth the morning.

Slow down

Farm stands

June is the height of the season. Pull over for berries and cut flowers — this is the corner of New Jersey that made us love it here.
